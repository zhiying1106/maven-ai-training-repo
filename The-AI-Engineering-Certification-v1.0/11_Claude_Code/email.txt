Hi team!

Marge here, owner of Whiskerful Wonders Cat Café (14 resident cats, roughly 400
anxious cat parents in our loyalty program, and one very tired me).

Here's my problem: every single day someone messages our shop account asking
things like "Mr. Bigglesworth ate a grape, is he going to die??" or "my kitten's
nose feels warm, is that a fever?" I love these people, but I am one woman with
a milk frother, not a 24/7 feline hotline.

So I want to hire you to build me a little web app. Working title:
"Whiskers, the Cat Health Assistant." Here's my wish list:

1. A chat page. Just a friendly chat window in the browser where people type a
   question and get an answer. Nothing fancy to install — a website.

2. It should feel alive. When Whiskers is "thinking," I want the answer to
   appear word by word, like it's typing back. My customers are impatient
   (they learned it from their cats).

3. It needs to remember the conversation. If someone says "my cat is Biscuit,
   he's 12" and then asks "is tuna okay for him?", Whiskers should still know
   who Biscuit is. No goldfish memory, please. Wrong animal entirely.

4. Real answers, not vibes. Two things it MUST be able to actually look up
   rather than guess:
   - Vitals check: if a customer says "her temperature is 39.8 and she's
     breathing 40 times a minute," Whiskers should check those numbers against
     real normal ranges for cats and say what's high, low, or fine.
   - Food & plant safety: "can cats eat cheese?" "he chewed on the lily!!" —
     I want a proper toxic / caution / safe lookup, with the lily one treated
     like the emergency it truly is.

5. Guardrails, guardrails, guardrails:
   - Whiskers is NOT a vet and should say so. General guidance only, and it
     should tell people to call an actual veterinarian for anything serious.
   - For scary symptoms (can't breathe, can't pee, ate a lily, collapsed…)
     it should lead with "GO TO THE EMERGENCY VET" before anything else.
   - Cats only! If someone asks about their iguana or their crypto portfolio,
     Whiskers should politely steer them back to cats.
   - And it should not be able to poke around your servers or the internet —
     give it exactly the two lookup abilities above and nothing else. I saw a
     documentary about rogue AI. Not in my café.

6. If something breaks, no scary error screens. A polite "sorry, try again in
   a moment — and call a vet if it's urgent" is exactly the tone I want.

Budget: I will pay in money AND unlimited oat-milk lattes AND you may pet
Chairman Meow (he bites, but affectionately).

Can you have something I can click around in soon? I'd love to see it answer a
real question like "my cat ate chocolate, how bad is it?" from start to finish.

Purrfully yours,
Marge Pawlowski
